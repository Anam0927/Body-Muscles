Arms

Biceps

Aids the flexion of the elbow and rotation of the forearm

Triceps

Aids the extension of the forearm using the elbow joint and adduction of the arm using the shoulder joints

Forearm

Aids the extension of the elbow joint to the hands. It is composed of the ulna and radius bones

Abdomen

Transversus Abdominis

Most profound muscle and it tremendously affects body posture as it holds up the spine

Internal Obliques

Located on the sides and are not as deep as the transversus abdominis

External Obliques

They are located on each side of the torso. They aid in rotation and support of the spine

Rectus Abdominis

Located in the front of the abdomen and is the muscle that we all visibly notice. It is responsible for creating the six-pack figure and provide the body's posture

Back

Trapezius

Also known as traps, are the muscles that connect the deltoids to the back. Aids in the support of the weight of the arm along with the rotation and retraction of the shoulder blade

Rhomboids

Also known as the middle back. Aids in squeezing the shoulder blades together and create stability in the shoulder muscles

Latissimus Dorsi

Also known as Lats, helps with the pulling up and pulling down movement of the arm and back, having the elbow remain close to the body

Erector Spinae

Also known as lower back, aid in stabilizing the spine and core muscles

Glutes

Glutes are one of the biggest muscle groups in the body. They help keep the pelvis in a stabilized position and provide aid to rotate and extend the hip

Legs

Quadriceps

Allow for the extension of the leg and knee along with the flexion of the thigh and knee

Hamstrings

Aid the movement of the lower body through the hip. Permits the flexion of the knee and keeps the hamstrings and pelvis stabilized

Calves

Aids in the lifting movement of the heels
